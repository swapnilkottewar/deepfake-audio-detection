About AudioSleuth

We are dedicated to building a more trustworthy digital world by combating audio-based disinformation.

Our Mission

In an era of rapid AI advancement, the line between real and synthetic media is blurring. Deepfake audio poses a significant threat to personal security, corporate integrity, and public trust. AudioSleuth was founded by a team of AI researchers and engineers who believe in transparency and accountability.

Our mission is to provide accessible, state-of-the-art tools for journalists, corporations, and the public to detect and verify the authenticity of digital audio, ensuring that voice remains a reliable source of truth.

Our Technology

We use a multi-layered detection engine, powered by the industry-leading Wave2Vec model.

Core Model: Wave2Vec 2.0

At the heart of AudioSleuth is a fine-tuned implementation of Wave2Vec 2.0, a self-supervised model. Unlike traditional models that rely on spectrograms (visual representations of audio), Wave2Vec learns directly from the raw audio waveform.

This allows it to capture subtle, low-level artifacts, distortions, and inconsistencies in the audio signal that are imperceptible to the human ear but are tell-tale signs of AI generation.

How It Works

  1. Step 1: Audio Preprocessing

    Your uploaded file is loaded, resampled to 16,000 Hz, and converted to a single channel (mono) to match the model's training data.

  2. Step 2: Feature Extraction

    The raw waveform is fed into the model's feature extractor, which converts the audio into a rich, contextualized set of vector representations.

  3. Step 3: Classification

    A classification head, trained on datasets like ASVspoof, analyzes these vectors and outputs a final "REAL" (bonafide) or "FAKE" (spoof) score with high confidence.
